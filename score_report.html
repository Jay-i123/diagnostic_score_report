Assessment Methodology

Cognitive Diagnostic Model

This assessment uses a DINA (Deterministic Inputs, Noisy "And" Gate) cognitive diagnostic model. The model classifies mastery of latent attributes (subskills) based on item response patterns, computing independent posterior probabilities for each subskill at each assessment point.

Non-Linear Language Acquisition

Language acquisition is not a linear process. Students may show regression in some areas while progressing in others, and growth may plateau before accelerating. The model accounts for this by computing independent posterior probabilities at each assessment point rather than assuming monotonic growth.

Norming Population

Stanine scores for the EAL/D diagnostic are normed against EAL/D students in Australian schools (Band B, Years 7–9).

EAL/D Diagnostic Interpretation & Recommendations

Reading / Viewing — Profile: [1,1,1,0,0] ▼

Profile: Word Recognition, Comprehension of Main Ideas, and Locating Specific Information mastered; Inferential Comprehension and Critical Text Analysis not mastered.

Linh can recognise and process vocabulary at Year 9 level, identify the main idea through skimming, and locate explicitly stated information. However, she is unable to reliably make inferences from text (integrating information, reading between the lines, reconstructing discourse coherence) or critically analyse rhetorical purpose and writer's stance.

Priority Target: Inferential Comprehension. Because the attributes are hierarchical, Inferential Comprehension must be addressed before Critical Text Analysis can develop.

Recommended Strategies:

  • Use short, engaging texts (reviews, forum posts, emails) and model the think-aloud process for inferential reading.
  • Explicitly teach bridging inferences: "What does the writer suggest but not directly say?"
  • Use the Reorder Paragraph activity format to develop discourse-level inference skills.
  • Delay Critical Text Analysis instruction until Inferential Comprehension reaches mastery (posterior probability ≥ 0.60).
Listening — Profile: [1,1,0,0] ▼

Profile: Aural Word Recognition and Comprehension of Main Ideas mastered; Extracting Specific Information and Inferential Comprehension not mastered.

Linh can segment the speech stream and identify the overall topic of spoken texts. However, she struggles to extract specific details from audio and cannot yet make propositional or pragmatic inferences from spoken input.

Priority Target: Extracting Specific Information. The hierarchy requires this before Inferential Comprehension.

Recommended Strategies:

  • Use short audio clips (voicemails, announcements, news snippets) with explicit information-extraction tasks.
  • Control speech rate (150–170 wpm for Year 9) and use single-play protocol to build real-time processing.
  • Avoid inferential comprehension tasks until Extracting Specific Information reaches mastery.
Speaking — Profile: [1,0,0,0,0] ▼

Profile: Only Pronunciation mastered.

Linh can produce individual phonemes and phonemic contrasts intelligibly. However, she has not yet mastered Stress & Intonation (stress, rhythm, connected speech), which blocks all higher-level speaking attributes.

Priority Target: Stress & Intonation — specifically lexical stress placement, unstressed syllable reduction, and connected speech processes (linking, elision).

Dynamic Assessment Insight: On Language Use in Context tasks, Linh scored 0 on Phase 1 (independent) for face-threatening speech acts (requesting from a teacher, declining an invitation) but succeeded at Tier 2 mediation (explicit feedback) for requesting and Tier 3 (model provided) for declining. Simple formulaic acts (congratulating, thanking) were produced independently. This suggests the pragmatic knowledge is partially available but complex politeness strategies remain in the ZPD. However, Stress & Intonation must be addressed first.

Recommended Strategies:

  • Passage read-aloud activities focusing on multisyllabic stress (proFESsor, parTICular, ecoNOMical).
  • Audio-model reproduction exercises: "Say it the way the speaker said it" to build connected speech.
  • Delay Spoken Fluency and Language Use in Context work until Stress & Intonation is established.
Writing — Profile: [M, M, E, D, D, A] ▼

Profile: Mastered: Vocabulary, Grammar. Emerging: Text Structure & Cohesion. Developing: Creating Texts, Language for Audience & Purpose. Absent: Persuasion & Rhetoric.

Vocabulary — Linh demonstrates strong subject-specific and academic vocabulary for Year 9, including appropriate word choice across familiar text types. Grammar — Sentence structure, verb forms, and tenses are at mastery level, though persistent L1-influenced errors with articles, prepositions, and complex tense sequences are expected for Vietnamese L1 speakers and may continue to surface at lower levels. Despite both being mastered, her writing lacks logical sequencing and cohesion. With a single implicit prompt from the interlocutor, Text Structure & Cohesion improved significantly (ZPD = 3), indicating this skill is on the verge of independent mastery.

Priority Targets: Text Structure & Cohesion (Emerging — scaffold and it will consolidate quickly), then Creating Texts.

Dynamic Assessment Insight: Text Structure & Cohesion responded to implicit mediation — when the interlocutor simply expressed confusion about the order of events, Linh self-corrected and reorganised. Creating Texts required explicit mediation (direct prompt to address missing content points). Language for Audience & Purpose only responded to maximal mediation (model provided). Persuasion & Rhetoric did not respond to any level of mediation — this attribute is currently beyond the ZPD and should not be targeted until the prerequisites consolidate.

Recommended Strategies:

  • For Text Structure & Cohesion: Use communicative writing tasks (text message exchanges, emails) with implicit prompts that model cohesion. Linh is very close to mastery here.
  • For Creating Texts: Use checklists and content-point scaffolds. Explicitly teach students to review prompts before writing.
  • Delay Language for Audience & Purpose instruction until Creating Texts is stable. Delay Persuasion & Rhetoric entirely.

What You Can Do at Home

Simple activities to support Linh's English development outside school.

Reading / Viewing

Encourage Linh to read short articles, book reviews, or blog posts in English. Ask her: "What do you think the writer really means?" to help build her ability to read between the lines.

Listening

Watch English TV shows or YouTube videos together with subtitles. Pause and ask specific questions: "What time did they say?" or "How many people were there?" to practice catching details.

Speaking

Practice reading aloud together — even 5 minutes a day helps. Focus on copying the rhythm and stress patterns of native speakers. Try audiobook + read-along activities.

Writing

Encourage Linh to write short messages, diary entries, or emails in English. Ordering ideas clearly is her next step — ask her to plan what she wants to say before she writes.
